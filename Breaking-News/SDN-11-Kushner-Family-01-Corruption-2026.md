## Criminal Considerations

Federal And International

The following analysis details potential criminal violations under US  federal law and international statutes based on the documented official and private conduct of _Jared Kushner_ during the 2025-2026 period.

These acts focus on the intersection of his role as _Special Envoy for Peace_ (appointed February 19, 2026) and his leadership of _Affinity Partners_.

### Conflict Of Interest And Self-Dealing

(18 USC. Section 208)

- Federal law prohibits executive branch employees from participating personally and substantially in government matters that have a direct and predictable effect on their financial interests or those of their business associates.

- Kushner's dual role as a chief negotiator for Gaza redevelopment and the founder of a firm seeking 5 billion dollars from the same Middle Eastern governments involved in those negotiations presents a prima facie case for self-dealing.

- The criminal statute applies to any "official act" taken while drawing a federal salary or serving in a designated official capacity, such as a Special Envoy.

### Foreign Agents Registration Act

(FARA) Violations (22 USC. Section 611 et seq.)

- FARA requires individuals acting as agents of foreign principals in a political or quasi-political capacity to make public disclosure of their relationship and activities.

- Senate investigations led by _Ron Wyden_ have characterized Affinity Partners as a potential "compensation scheme" designed to circumvent FARA by masking political influence as private investment.

- Acting as a US  official while receiving massive "management fees" (e.g., 87 million dollars from the Saudi government) without generating profit suggests the payments may be for influence rather than commercial investment.

### Honest Services Fraud

(18 USC. Section 1346)

- This statute criminalizes schemes to deprive the public of the intangible right to "honest services" through bribes or kickbacks.

- If the 2 billion dollar investment from the Saudi _Public Investment Fund_ or the subsequent 5 billion dollar fundraising effort is linked to specific diplomatic concessions (such as the Gaza Board of Peace terms), it may constitute a violation of honest services.

### Money Laundering

(18 USC. Sections 1956 and 1957)

- The movement of foreign government funds into a US  investment firm under the guise of "management fees" for a firm with no established track record can be scrutinized as laundering if the funds are derived from "specified unlawful activity" or intended to promote such activity.

- The Senate Finance Committee's finding that 99 percent of Affinity’s assets come from foreign sources increases the risk of the firm being used as a conduit for foreign government money to reach US  political figures.

### RICO

(Racketeer Influenced And Corrupt Organizations Act)

(18 USC. Section 1961)

- A RICO charge would require proving an "enterprise" (potentially the Trump administration and Affinity Partners) engaged in a "pattern of racketeering activity."

- Predicate acts could include repeated instances of wire fraud (using electronic communications to solicit funds based on official influence) and bribery.

- The coordinated efforts between Kushner and other appointed officials, such as _Steve Witkoff_ or _Richard Grenell_, to facilitate these deals while in government roles could satisfy the enterprise requirement.

### Misprision Of Felony

(18 USC. Section 4)

- This applies to officials who have actual knowledge of the commission of a felony (such as a bribery or a FARA violation) and fail to report it, or take affirmative steps to conceal it.

- Appointed officials who facilitated Kushner’s "unofficial" meetings in Davos or the negotiation of the Gaza Board of Peace while knowing of his 5 billion dollar solicitation could be held liable.

### Obstruction Of Justice

(18 USC. Section 1512)

- Any attempt to influence or impede the Senate Finance Committee’s investigation into Affinity Partners or the _Office of Government Ethics (OGE)_ review of Kushner’s financial disclosures falls under this category.

- Tampering with witnesses or providing false statements to Congressional investigators regarding the identity of the "fifth mystery investor" would constitute obstruction.

### Accessory After The Fact

(18 USC. Section 3)

- This applies to individuals who assist Kushner in hindering his apprehension or trial by concealing the nature of his foreign financial ties after a crime has been committed.

### Aiding And Abetting

(18 USC. Section 2)

- This charge applies to any person who assists in the commission of the aforementioned offenses, such as legal advisors who knowingly draft "loophole" agreements to hide foreign influence.

# Significant Ethical Breaches

Allegations of significant ethical breaches involving _Jared Kushner_, President Donald Trump's son-in-law and Special Envoy for Peace.

Reports indicate that while serving as a high-level US negotiator in the Middle East, Kushner is simultaneously seeking to raise over 5 billion dollars for his private equity firm, _Affinity Partners_, from foreign governments.

Critics and ethics watchdogs argue this creates an unprecedented blurring of lines between official diplomatic duties and private profit-seeking, specifically highlighting potential conflicts of interest with sovereign wealth funds in the Middle East.

## Key Points

### Fundraising Target

- Kushner is actively seeking more than 5 billion dollars from foreign nations for his firm, Affinity Partners.

### Official Dual Role

- The fundraising efforts are occurring while Kushner serves as a chief negotiator and Special Envoy for Peace in the Middle East.

### Davos Activities

- Kushner allegedly used his position as an official US delegate at the World Economic Forum in Davos to hold private meetings with business leaders regarding his investment firm.

### Saudi Connection

- Affinity Partners previously received a 2 billion dollar investment from the Saudi Public Investment Fund (PIF) soon after the first Trump administration ended

- new discussions with PIF are reportedly underway.

### Broken Promise

- The report notes that in December 2024, Kushner claimed he would not need to raise capital for the firm for the next four years.

### Ethical Scrutiny

- Organizations like CREW note that Kushner's official appointment triggers mandatory public financial disclosures, which are intended to identify and resolve these specific conflicts of interest.

## Corruption

The mind-boggling nature of these allegations stems from the intersection of federal ethics laws and international diplomacy.

In the United States, the Conflict of Interest Statute (18 USC. Section 208) generally prohibits executive branch employees from participating in matters that affect their personal financial interests.

Kushner's situation is unique because he transitioned from an unofficial advisor to a formal Special Envoy, which brings his activities under stricter federal oversight.

The primary concern is quid pro quo corruption: the possibility that US foreign policy or diplomatic favors could be exchanged for investments into Kushner's private firm.
